Hard Drive:

The hard drive houses the operating system, the files, and programs an individual keeps on the computer. There are different types of hard drives such as the hard disk drive and the solid-state drive. Solid-state drives also known as SSD, store information in nonvolatile memory chips and have no moving pieces. Solid state drives tend to be more durable, quieter, and use low power, but do tend to cost more.

The hard disk drives also known as HDD, read and write information using magnetics on multiple disks. The speed in which the disk will write this information is 5400 RPM to 15000 RPM. The cost of hard disk drives is considerably lower than solid-state drives, but hard disk drives are slower in comparison. When a person is building their own personal computer there are different companies selling hard disk drives such as Seagate, Western Digital, and Kingston. Companies selling solid-state drives are Samsung, Crucial, and Western Digital.

Solid-state drives will be faster at writing information whereas hard disk drives will be slower with noticeable differences. An individual building their computer would be able to install solid-state drives and hard disk drives. Usually with most computer cases, they have multiple slots to install multiple hard disk drives or solid-state drives. When a person is buying a pre-built or creating a custom build, they will still have a good range of solid-state drives and hard disk drives to choose from. An individual purchasing a pre-built would be able to upgrade if there are additional slots available to do so.
